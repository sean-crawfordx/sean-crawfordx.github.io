🧠 Neuroplasticity and Growth Mindset 🌱

Until recently, people believed our brains were “set in stone” at birth, fixed and unable to change. But now, thanks to science, we know that’s not true. Our brains are flexible and constantly change based on what we do, think, and learn, This is called neuroplasticity.
Our mindset is how we think about our abilities which can either help or hold us back.

🔁 What Is Neuroplasticity?

Neuroplasticity is your brain’s ability to change and grow. Every time you learn something new, your brain creates or strengthens connections between brain cells (called neurons).
Have you ever struggled with a skill, like playing an instrument or riding a bike, and then grown better over time? That’s neuroplasticity in action.
The more you practice, the stronger the brain pathways become. You’re building a stronger brain.

💡 Why It Matters

You’re not stuck with the brain you were born with.

You can constantly improve at any age.

People can recover from strokes or injuries or manage things like ADHD, depression, or learning difficulties.

🚀 How To Boost Neuroplasticity

Practice regularly + repetition = stronger brain connections.

Challenge yourself to something new!

Sleep well your brain rewires while you rest.

Stay curious, ask questions and explore ideas.

🌟 What Is a Growth Mindset?

A growth mindset is the belief that your abilities and intelligence can improve with effort and learning. This idea comes from psychologist Carol Dweck. A fixed mindset, on the other hand, is the belief that you’re either smart or not, and that’s it.

🎯 Why It Matters

If you believe you can grow and learn you’re likelier to try harder, bounce back from mistakes, and feel proud of your progress, not just perfection.

🧩 What Surprised Me

I realised how often we accidentally give people fixed mindset messages

“You’re so smart!” or “You’re not a math person.”

They sound nice but can make people afraid to fail.

💬 How I’ll Use It

I’ll remind myself that effort and mistakes are part of learning. I won’t say, “I’m just bad at this.” I’ll say, “I’m not good at this YET”.
I’ll keep pushing, even when it’s tough — because that’s when my brain is growing.

📝 How This Shapes My Learning Strategy

Knowing this is helping me change how I learn.

Celebrating my effort, not just results.

Reflect more often to help my brain build strong connections.

Always remind myself that I can change and grow, and I will!
