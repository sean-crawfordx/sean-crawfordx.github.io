🤖❌ You Can’t Copy and Paste Human Skills


🧠💬 Human Skills Reflection


👤 What have I learned about myself and my ego?

I take feedback personally sometimes, but I’m learning that it's okay to feel things.
I can step back and see feedback as a way to grow, not a personal attack. I don’t have to be in control, but I like to know where I’m heading.
I'm learning to slow down and trust the process. I’ve avoided asking for help, turns out that came from not being open about how I feel.
I don’t need to fear questions. Questions are how I grow.


💖 The role of values, empathy, and self-awareness in learning and coding

Values guide my decisions and how I work with others. Knowing what matters to me helps me work with purpose. Empathy helps me collaborate better.
It’s not just about understanding code, it’s about understanding people too. Self-awareness helps me catch my habits and adjust them.
If I get frustrated or distracted, I can pause, reflect, and reset.


🤯 What surprised me the most?

I wanted to push myself into the human side of this journey, learning to listen, speak up, and be more confident around others.
Programming is a people’s job. These skills make everything easier, not harder. I’m genuinely surprised at how open and confident I’m becoming.


🧗‍♂️ What were the most challenging parts?

Being open and not knowing what’s expected freaked me out a bit 😅.
It was hard to share my thoughts honestly, especially if I felt unsure or emotional. But doing it made me feel more connected and supported.


🤔 Why is a coding bootcamp teaching Human Skills?

Because coding is teamwork. No matter how good I get at JavaScript, if I can’t listen, communicate, or reflect, I’ll struggle in real jobs.
Human Skills make me a better teammate, a better learner, and even a better problem solver.


🚫 Was it a waste of time?

Nope. 👎 My objective is to learn how to communicate with people, and with myself.
Learning to code along the way is a bonus.

Computers can’t smile. YET. 😏
