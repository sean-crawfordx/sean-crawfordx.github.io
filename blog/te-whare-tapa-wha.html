🌿 Te Whare Tapa Whā

Te Whare Tapa Whā is a Māori model of wellbeing created by Tā Mason Durie in 1984.
It uses the image of a wharenui (meeting house) to show how wellbeing has four walls – and each one needs to be strong for us to feel balanced.


The 4 Walls of Wellbeing

🕊️ Taha Wairua – Spiritual wellbeing. Connecting with meaning, values, or something sacred.

🧠 Taha Hinengaro – Mental & emotional wellbeing. Managing our thoughts and feelings, and how we cope with stress.

🏃‍♂️ Taha Tinana – Physical wellbeing. Caring for our bodies through food, movement, and rest.

👨‍👩‍👧‍👦 Taha Whānau – Family & Social wellbeing. Relationships, support systems, and the people who matter.

🌏 Whenua – Land, roots, environment. The land grounds us — nature connects us to ourselves and others.

🌱 Why This Matters

Dev Academy supports the whole person — not just learning code.
Te Whare Tapa Whā helps us look after our hauora (wellbeing) while we study, grow and support each other.

Taha hinengaro (Mental & Emotional Wellbeing)
